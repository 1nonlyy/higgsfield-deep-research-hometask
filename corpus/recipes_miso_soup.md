# Miso soup with wakame and tofu — pantry staples

Miso soup belongs to the daily rhythm of many Japanese households and adapts well to Western pantries if a few ingredients are kept on hand. Start with dashi made from kombu steep plus bonito flakes, or use instant dashi granules sparingly to avoid oversalting.

Hydrate cut **wakame** in cold water; it expands quickly. Cubed silken tofu should be added gently after the dashi reaches a bare simmer—boiling violently can fracture the curd. Whisk miso paste with a small amount of hot broth in a separate bowl to dissolve lumps before combining, then remove the pot from direct heat to preserve aromatic compounds.

Optional garnishes include sliced scallion, shichimi togarashi, or a few drops of sesame oil. This recipe page is culinary **noise** for corpus breadth and should not be mistaken for laboratory procedures despite shared words like “heat” and “solutions.”
