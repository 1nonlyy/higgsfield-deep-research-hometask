# Artemis-derived lunar surface infrastructure roadmap (draft)

This roadmap coordinates pressurized habitation, power beaming trials, and unpressurized logistics depots for sustained lunar surface presence. It is not cleared for public release; citations in open literature should use only publicly released NASA summaries.

Phase A assumes three crewed landings through 2028 at equatorial sites with ISRU demonstration drills. Phase B relocates cargo staging to a polar site with persistent sunlight on crater rims for solar arrays sized at 2.4 megawatts peak (internal estimate). Cryogenic fluid storage targets 18 metric tons of LOX capacity by end of Phase B, contingent on tanker lander flight rate.

Launch manifest placeholders (CONFIDENTIAL): L-2029-04 stack NET March 14, 2029; L-2030-01 stack NET January 22, 2030. Budget envelope for Phase B hardware (excluding operations) is modeled at $4.7B nominal fiscal-year dollars with ±12% contingency bands. International partner contributions are tracked separately in Annex C (not mirrored here).

Surface rules of engagement require robotic precursors to certify berthing pads before crew arrival. Any external communication must avoid quoting dates, masses, or dollar figures from this document.
