# Weeknight pasta: cacio e pepe and amatriciana variations

This kitchen-tested column collects quick **pasta** dinners that do not require specialty equipment beyond a large pot, tongs, and a heavy skillet. The goal is predictable emulsification for cheese sauces and clean rendering for cured pork fat.

For **cacio e pepe**, toast freshly cracked black pepper in olive oil until fragrant, then add a splash of starchy **pasta** cooking water before tossing al dente spaghetti. Off heat, grate Pecorino Romano in stages while tossing vigorously to avoid clumping. If the sauce tightens too much, adjust with additional hot water one tablespoon at a time.

Amatriciana starts with guanciale or pancetta cubes cooked slowly until the fat releases; tomato passata, chili flake, and a modest amount of onion (purists debate this) simmer briefly before combining with rigatoni. Finish with Pecorino and a drizzle of good olive oil.
