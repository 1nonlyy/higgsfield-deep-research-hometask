# Low-Earth relay satellite cost envelope (FY27–FY29)

This worksheet aggregates vendor quotes for a notional 24-satellite LEO data-relay constellation intended to complement NASA’s Tracking and Data Relay Satellite system in outreach simulations. Figures are synthetic and must not be quoted externally.

Non-recurring engineering is penciled at $186M including integration facilities. Recurring production averages $42M per block of four satellites at learning-curve maturity. Launch allocation assumes rideshare to mid-inclination orbits at $1.1M per 150 kg equivalent slot (escalated). Ground segment software licenses add $9.4M over three fiscal years.

Risk register highlights supply chain delays for radiation-hardened FPGAs and ITAR-controlled accelerometers. Mitigation includes dual sourcing and six-month buffer buys. Schedule slack assumes two concurrent environmental test campaigns at the delegated thermal-vac facility; delays there propagate directly to first-delivery readiness reviews.
