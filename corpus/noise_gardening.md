# Raised bed gardening: soil mixes for tomatoes and peppers

Building raised beds improves drainage on clay-heavy yards and extends the growing season because elevated soil warms slightly faster in spring. A common three-part blend combines compost, peat or coconut coir, and aeration chips such as perlite or rice hulls in roughly equal volumes by bulk, adjusted for local rainfall.

Tomatoes benefit from consistent calcium availability to reduce blossom end rot; crushed eggshells help slowly but are not a substitute for liming if pH is wrong. Test soil pH toward 6.2–6.8 for nightshades. Peppers appreciate warm roots; black plastic mulch can accelerate early growth in cool climates, then be removed once stable heat arrives.

Irrigation timing matters more than duration: deep, less frequent watering encourages deeper roots compared with shallow sprinkles.
