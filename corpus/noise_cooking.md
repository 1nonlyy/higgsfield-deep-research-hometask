# Cast iron skillet maintenance and weeknight stir-fry timing

Home cooks often debate whether to soap a cast iron pan. Modern mild detergents remove acidic residues without stripping polymerized oil if rinses are brief and the pan is reheated to dry. For stir-fries, the sequence matters: aromatics first, proteins second after a hot sear, vegetables last to preserve crunch.

Oil smoke points become relevant when combining techniques. Peanut and refined avocado oils tolerate high heat for blistered green beans; butter finishes sauces after lowering heat. Wok hei is difficult on domestic ranges; a ripping hot carbon steel skillet preheated for several minutes approximates some of the effect.
